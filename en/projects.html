Exploratory analysis of cancellation reasons & retention levers
BNP Paribas · E-Business

Goal: Identify major causes of cancellation, analyze their evolution over time, segment the most exposed profiles and formulate concrete retention levers, actionable by business teams.

Analytical approach (Python & BI):
  • Data preparation, cleaning and reliability in Python (pandas, numpy): quality control, duplicate and missing value management, creation of key derived variables (seniority, contract lifetime, cancellation timing).
  • Structuring and semantic grouping of cancellation reasons using business rules and text processing in Python (regex, pandas) to build a stable and exploitable nomenclature.
  • Descriptive, cross and temporal analyses conducted in Python (pandas, matplotlib, plotly) to identify dominant reasons, associated customer profiles and evolution signals over time.
  • Segmentation of cancelled profiles via clustering methods in Python (scikit-learn) to highlight behavioral patterns and priority populations to address.
  • Presentation and highlighting of results in a Power BI dashboard, fed by enriched and prepared datasets in Python.
  • Translation of analyses into operational retention recommendations (pricing, customer experience, digital journeys, targeted retention offers).
Deliverables:
  • Enriched and documented datasets from Python processing
  • Exploratory Power BI dashboard (reasons, profiles, temporal dynamics)
  • Documented and industrializable data model
  • Analytical summary with targeted retention recommendations

Stack: Python (pandas, numpy, scikit-learn, matplotlib, plotly) · Power BI · Exploratory analysis · Customer segmentation · Data quality

Automatic email ticket processing agent — Automation & AI
Personal project / automation exercise

Goal: Develop a software agent capable of automating the processing of tickets received by email, from reading messages to their classification, prioritization and centralization in a tracking tool, to simulate a professional ticket management system.

What I did:
  • Design of an automatic email processing software agent.
  • Implementation of a pipeline allowing automatic reading of tickets received by email.
  • Analysis and interpretation of message content to extract key information.
  • Automatic classification of tickets by business category.
  • Assignment of an urgency level for each ticket, from most trivial to most critical.
  • Automatic feeding of a shared Google Sheet, structured by categories, to centralize and exploit processed tickets.
  • Simulation of operation close to an automated professional ticketing tool.
Operating principle:
  • Automatic reading of a mailbox containing structured tickets.
  • Analysis of subject and content of each email.
  • Ticket categorization (support, technical, administrative, etc.).
  • Urgency level assessment according to problem impact.
  • Generation of a ticket summary.
  • Automatic writing of key information in the corresponding tab of the Google Sheet.
Nature of processed data:
  • Mailbox containing several hundred emails simulating user tickets.
  • Each ticket includes a subject and textual content.
  • Varied cases representing real support situations (technical, access, user requests, incidents, bugs).
Project objectives:
  • Automate a time-consuming manual process.
  • Structure and centralize information from unstructured emails.
  • Prioritize requests according to their urgency level.
  • Facilitate steering and ticket tracking via a single support.
Project nature:
  • Personal project / automation exercise focused on data and AI.
  • No formal business deliverable: the goal is the design and operation of the solution.
  • Project focused on simulating real cases encountered in IT or support professional environments.
